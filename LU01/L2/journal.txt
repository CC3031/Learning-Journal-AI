LLM - predicts what word comes next for any piece of text. Assigns probability for each best word.

The way an LLM behaves depends on what it's trained on.

Continuous values / parameters - the data the model is trained on. Large language models can have hundreds of billions of these parameters.

Transformer model architecture - basically all LLMs use it. Introduced by "Attention Is All You Need" article by Google in 2017.

AI has emergent abilities. Originally used to translate English to German, somehow was capable of doing other things.
How is an AI able to summarize a sentence?

Weak/Narrow AI - only good at specialized things.

Tokenizing - prompt text is chopped into tokens (3-5 letters).
